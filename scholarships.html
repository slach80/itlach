Scholarships & Financial Aid

Music talent scholarships, merit aid, and competition prize paths for Isaac's conservatory and university admissions journey.

Priority Actions

What Matters Most Now

The three things that will most impact Isaac's scholarship eligibility over the next few years.

1
Build Competition Record
MTNA wins, Cliburn Junior, and regional placements are the strongest signal to conservatories for talent scholarships.
2
Maintain Strong GPA
3.9+ GPA keeps merit-based university scholarships (outside conservatories) on the table — especially at schools like UM Frost, FSU, KU.
3
Score High on SAT/ACT
Top test scores unlock academic merit aid at university music schools and broaden scholarship eligibility beyond talent-only awards.
Planning Timeline

Scholarship Roadmap

Key milestones from now through senior year of high school.

Now · 7th Grade (2025–26)
Build foundation
Enter first local competitions (MMTA, KC Music Club). Establish GPA discipline. Begin building recording portfolio with Eva Peng.
8th–9th Grade
Regional competition push
Target MTNA state-level entry. Explore pre-college programs (UMKC, Juilliard Pre-College, Colburn Pre-College). Take PSAT.
10th–11th Grade
National competitions + test prep
Target MTNA national round. Consider Cliburn Junior Competition. Begin SAT/ACT prep. Research audition requirements for all target schools.
12th Grade
Auditions & scholarship applications
Submit audition recordings. Apply for institutional talent scholarships. Apply for outside music scholarships. FAFSA + CSS Profile.
